The problem

Hip fractures account for an estimated £1.1 billion in the UK’s annual hospital costs. Due to an ageing population, the incidences of hip fracture are estimated to rise from 1.66 million in 1990 to 6.26 million by 2050, which will cause the cost to increase dramatically. This cost is due to recurrent hospitalizations and orthopaedic complications related to the delay in post-operative physiotherapy following fragility hip fracture surgery. The bad quality of the physiotherapy service is the problem we want to solve.
